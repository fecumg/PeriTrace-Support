Frequently asked

Questions, answered

PeriTrace requires an iPhone that supports ARKit.

iPhone models with LiDAR are recommended for a smoother experience and more precise captures.

PeriTrace supports iOS 18.6 and later.

PeriTrace provides a pattern-oriented management system. Adding a new pattern means adding a pattern set that may later contain up to 40 pattern pieces. The limited pattern piece quantity preserves smooth user experience. If your physical pattern contains more than 40 pieces, consider split them into multiple patterns in PeriTrace.

On the Home screen:

  • Add a new pattern: Tap the Add New note_add button at the bottom of the screen.
  • Rename or delete: swipe a pattern left or right to reveal the Rename edit and Delete delete buttons.
  • Rearrange the list: Long-press a pattern and drag it to a new position.
  • Open a pattern canvas Tap a pattern to enter its canvas, where its pieces live.

Open a pattern from the Home screen, then tap the Trace filter_center_focus button at the bottom right to open the AR camera. You may be asked to grant camera access.

PeriTrace currently allows you to trace one pattern piece each time you open the AR tracing camera.

In the camera view, move your phone slowly back and forth over a flat, horizontal surface until the reticle appears flat on the surface. You now can start tracing your pattern piece.

The current version of the app supports 2 tracing modes:

  • Vertex tracing: Aim the reticle at the outline or seam line and tap the Trace Vertices button, at the bottom right of the screen, to capture vertices one by one. Vertices will connect automatically. A dashed line will close the shape between your first and last vertices, so you do not need to close it manually.
  • Marking: Aim the reticle at a mark on the physical pattern piece and tap the Mark . Marks stand alone and never connect to vertices or other marks.
Contour detection: PeriTrace also features automatic pattern contour detection. This useful sub-feature of the Vertex Tracing mode automatically detects the outline of a pattern piece and quickly plots simplified vertices.

  • Activate contour detection: point the camera at a pattern piece and long-press the screen (for at least 0.5 seconds) to capture the outline's vertices. It may take a moment to capture the outline; moving your finger or releasing the press early will cancel the detection.

  • One at a time: The app can only detect one outline at a time.

  • High contrast: The pattern piece must contrast significantly with the background (e.g., a light pattern piece on a dark background, or vice versa). This ensures accuracy and prevents the camera from freezing due to vertex overloading.

NOTES:

* Optimizing camera position and angle:

PeriTrace uses AR to make pattern tracing effortless. You do not need to point the camera perfectly perpendicular to the surface.

However, iPhone models without LiDAR can sometimes struggle with surface calibration, which may cause captured contours and points to drift.

To maximize precision, follow these best practices:

  • Maintain distance: Keep your iPhone between 0.5 and 2 meters directly above the pattern.

  • Verify calibration: Ensure the reticle perfectly aligns with your physical surface before tracing.


Finishing or Cancelling a Trace:

  • Finish tracing: Tap the Done check button at the top right of the screen. You will return to pattern canvas, and the new pattern piece will appear at the center of the screen. If it overlaps with existing pieces, it will automatically move the right of them.

    • Minimum vertices: A new piece can only be saved when it has at least three vertices. The Done button will remain disabled until this requirement is met.

  • Cancel tracing: Tap the Dismiss keyboard_arrow_down button at the top left of the screen. You will return to pattern canvas without saving a new piece

PeriTrace offers an intuitive vector canvas featuring gesture support. Your layout arrangements are automatically preserved when exporting files.

Canvas Navigation:

  • Zoom & pan: Use pinch and drag gesture to move around the workspace.

Managing pattern pieces:

  • Move & rotate a piece: Drag a pattern piece to reposition it, or use two fingers to rotate it.
  • Delete a piece: Long-press a pattern piece, then tap the Delete button in the tooltip

Pro tip for high precision:

When doubting a traced pattern piece's accuracy, try capturing it a few times. Stack all the captured pieces on top of each other on the canvas to find the average outline.

NOTES:

A single pattern canvas can hold up to 40 pattern pieces. If you physical pattern has more than 40 pieces, split the project across multiple separate patterns on PeriTrace.

PeriTrace exports your final pattern layout at an exact 1:1 scale, completely preserving your canvas arrangement.

How to export:

  • Export button: In pattern canvas screen, the Export ios_share button is located at the bottom left the screen. This button will remain disabled until there's at least one piece in the canvas
  • Select your format: Tap the Export button to bring up the export sheet. Select your desired format to generate the file.
  • Choose a destination: A standard iOS Share Sheet will appears after you select a format. Choose where to send, save, or share your file.
  • Instant local backup: No matter what you choose in the Share Sheet, PeriTrace automatically saves or overwrites the file locally to:
    On My iPhone → PeriTrace → Exports
  • Preview your file: tap Quick Look visibility Quick Look button after exporting to instantly preview your exported file.

Supported export formats:

PDFUniversal layout format. Ideal for standard printing, vector graphics software, or apparel CAD systems.
DXFindustrial-standard CAD format. Dedicated specifically for pattern drafting and garment CAD software.
SVGScalable vector graphics format. Best for web use or editing in mainstream vector design programs.
